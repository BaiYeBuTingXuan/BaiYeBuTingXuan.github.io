Chu Kochen Honor College, Zhejiang University, China

I am an undergraduate student of The Mixed class 2020, Chu Kochen Honor College, Zhejiang University, China, supervised by Prof Rong Xiong.

Previously, my research focused on continuous path planner in autonomous driving. Using knowledge distillation, I improved the robustness and adaptability of a driving intention generator under extremely adverse weather, and published a conference paper in IEEE Real-time Computing and Robot 2023 with me as the first author. In this summer, my research lies on Weather-Shift on 3D-Cloud, that I attempt to construct network to simulate how adverse weather affects LIDAR sensor.

AI system with corresponding entities, e.g. Intelligent Robot, Autonomous Vehicle, always attracts me. In future, I tend to research more about embodied AI on autonomous driving. Specifically, I may spend more time on investigating how to real-time, low-consuming, high-robust represent environmental information around the vehicles. What's more, I want each of my findings to has a solid mathematical explanation.
